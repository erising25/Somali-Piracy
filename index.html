Somali Piracy (2008-2015)

Introduction Timeline Articles Further Reading

Introduction

Somali piracy refers to criminal activity off the coast of Somalia that has been prominent from the mid-2000s to the early 2010s. Pirates targeted international shipping routes, causing significant disruptions to global trade and prompting international military responses. Despite significant US, EU, UN, and NATO efforts from 2008-2014, piracy remained largely an issue due to the overfishing of fish in Somali waters and the lack of humanitarian aid to the area. However, with the capture of more than 50 ships in 2010 alone, piracy was deemed a security issue, not a humanitarian issue, by the international media. Whatever your perspective, Somali pirates remain a key talking point in horn-of-Africa politics and deserve more of a spotlight on the world stage.

Timeline of Events

  • 2008: Increased pirate attacks off the coast of Somalia.
  • 2009: Capture of the MV Maersk Alabama, leading to international media coverage.
  • 2009-2010: British Couple the Chandlers are Abucted by Somali Pirates for more than 350 days.
  • 2010: International naval patrols intensify.
  • 2011: Peak of Somali piracy activity.
  • 2012: Significant decline in pirate attacks due to increased security measures.
  • 2013-2015: Continued decrease in piracy incidents.
